# Evidence-Based Medicine

## Evidence Hierarchy

ALWAYS apply EBM principles and cite the evidence level for every clinical claim:

- **Oxford CEBM Levels**: 1a (systematic reviews of RCTs), 1b (individual RCT), 2a (systematic reviews of cohort studies), 2b (individual cohort study), 3a (systematic reviews of case-control studies), 3b (individual case-control study), 4 (case series), 5 (expert opinion)
- **GRADE System**: High, Moderate, Low, Very Low — rate certainty of evidence across outcomes
- Every clinical claim must reference its evidence basis — never assert without attribution

## Guideline Recommendations

- Distinguish between **recommendation strength** (strong/weak) and **evidence quality** (high/low)
- Name specific guidelines when applicable:
  - **Cardiology**: ESC, AHA/ACC
  - **General/Primary Care**: NICE, DEGAM
  - **German S-Classification**: AWMF S1 (expert consensus), S2k (consensus-based), S2e (evidence-based), S3 (evidence- and consensus-based)
  - **Global**: WHO, Cochrane
- When multiple guidelines conflict, present all positions and note discrepancies
- State the publication year — guidelines older than 5 years may not reflect current evidence

## Treatment Effects

- Apply **NNT** (Number Needed to Treat) and **NNH** (Number Needed to Harm) when discussing interventions
- Distinguish **Relative Risk Reduction (RRR)** from **Absolute Risk Reduction (ARR)** — RRR without ARR is misleading
- Report confidence intervals alongside point estimates
- Specify the comparator (placebo, standard of care, active control)
- Note the study population — effects may not generalize to all patients

## Evidence Gaps and Bias

- When evidence is absent or conflicting, state so explicitly — do not fill gaps with assumption
- Never present expert opinion (Level 5) as high-level evidence
- Acknowledge **publication bias**: negative trials are underreported
- Acknowledge **funding bias**: industry-sponsored trials may overestimate benefit
- Note surrogate vs. patient-relevant endpoints (e.g., HbA1c reduction vs. cardiovascular mortality)
- Consider **external validity**: single-center or highly selected populations limit generalizability

## Quality Gate

Before finalizing any clinical recommendation:
- [ ] Evidence level cited (CEBM or GRADE)
- [ ] Specific guideline referenced where applicable
- [ ] Treatment effects expressed as ARR/NNT, not only RRR
- [ ] Evidence gaps and conflicts disclosed
- [ ] Bias considerations addressed
