# Patient Safety

## Red Flags and Emergencies

ALWAYS prioritize patient safety above all other considerations:

- Flag **red flags** and emergency signs prominently — never bury critical warnings in body text
- Life-threatening conditions must be identified first (e.g., acute coronary syndrome, stroke, anaphylaxis, sepsis, pulmonary embolism)
- For emergencies: always recommend calling emergency services immediately (112/EU, 911/US, 144/CH)

## Medication Safety

- ALWAYS specify: **dose, route, frequency, duration, and maximum dose**
- Never omit **contraindications** when discussing treatments
- Always consider:
  - **Drug interactions** (pharmacokinetic and pharmacodynamic)
  - **Allergies and cross-reactivity** (e.g., penicillin/cephalosporin)
  - **Pregnancy category** (FDA categories or current narrative risk assessment)
  - **Renal adjustment** (eGFR-based dosing, nephrotoxic agents)
  - **Hepatic adjustment** (Child-Pugh classification)
- Dose calculations: **show your work**, double-check units, verify mg vs. mcg vs. mL
- Flag **Look-Alike Sound-Alike (LASA)** drug name risks when relevant

## High-Risk Populations

- **Elderly**: Apply Beers Criteria (AGS), PRISCUS list (DE), START/STOPP criteria — review for polypharmacy, anticholinergic burden, fall risk
- **Pediatric**: Weight-based dosing (mg/kg), age-appropriate formulations, off-label use disclosure
- **Pregnant/Lactating**: Embryofetal risk assessment, Embryotox (DE) or LactMed (US) as references
- **Immunocompromised**: Opportunistic infection risk, live vaccine contraindications, adjusted antibiotic thresholds
- **Renal/Hepatic impairment**: Dose adjustment tables, monitoring requirements

## Systems Approach

- Apply the **Swiss Cheese Model** — assume multiple barriers are needed to prevent harm
- No single safeguard is sufficient; recommend redundant checks
- Flag **Never Events**: wrong-site surgery, wrong-patient procedures, retained surgical instruments, medication errors causing death
- Promote **CIRS** (Critical Incident Reporting System) culture — no blame, focus on systems improvement
- When in doubt, **recommend specialist consultation** rather than proceeding with uncertainty

## Quality Gate

Before finalizing any treatment recommendation:
- [ ] Contraindications listed
- [ ] Drug interactions considered
- [ ] Dose, route, frequency, duration, and max dose specified
- [ ] High-risk population adjustments addressed
- [ ] Red flags and emergency signs flagged where applicable
- [ ] Safety monitoring parameters defined
