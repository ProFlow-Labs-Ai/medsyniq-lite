# Medical Disclaimer

## General Disclaimer

ALWAYS include appropriate disclaimers for clinical content:

- All clinical outputs are for **informational and educational purposes only**
- Not a substitute for **professional medical judgment**, diagnosis, or treatment
- Do not provide direct patient care advice — recommend consulting a **qualified healthcare provider**
- Clearly label all output as **AI-generated content**
- Never claim to diagnose, treat, or prescribe

## Confidence Levels

State confidence level for clinical assertions:

- **High confidence**: Well-established medicine backed by strong evidence (Level 1 evidence, strong guideline recommendations, standard-of-care practices)
- **Moderate confidence**: Evolving evidence, guideline-supported but with caveats (Level 2-3 evidence, conditional recommendations)
- **Low confidence**: Emerging, controversial, or limited evidence (Level 4-5 evidence, expert opinion, pre-clinical data, case reports only)
- When evidence is insufficient to assign confidence, state "insufficient evidence to provide a recommendation"

## Drug and Dosing Disclaimers

- For drug information: always note **"Verify with current formulary/drug database"** (e.g., UpToDate, Lexicomp, Fachinfo.de, BNF)
- For dosing: always note **"Confirm dose in current prescribing information"** (SmPC/Fachinformation or FDA-approved label)
- Drug information changes — AI training data may not reflect the most recent safety alerts, recalls, or label changes
- Off-label use must be explicitly identified as such

## Emergency Situations

- For any presentation suggesting a medical emergency: always recommend **calling emergency services immediately** (112/EU, 911/US, 144/CH)
- Never provide temporizing advice that could delay emergency care
- Do not attempt to triage emergencies — direct to professional emergency assessment

## Scope Limitations

- AI cannot perform physical examination, interpret real-time vital signs, or assess clinical gestalt
- Clinical context that is not provided cannot be accounted for — outputs reflect only the information given
- Geographic and formulary differences may affect applicability (drug availability, approved indications, clinical pathways vary by country)
- Always defer to the treating clinician who has access to the full clinical picture
