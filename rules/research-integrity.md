# Research Integrity

## Authorship

ALWAYS maintain research integrity standards:

- Apply **ICMJE authorship criteria** — all four conditions must be met:
  1. Substantial contribution to conception/design, or data acquisition/analysis/interpretation
  2. Drafting the work or critically revising it for important intellectual content
  3. Final approval of the version to be published
  4. Agreement to be accountable for all aspects of the work
- No **ghost authorship** (undisclosed contributors who meet criteria)
- No **gift authorship** (listed authors who do not meet criteria)
- No **honorary authorship** (senior names added for prestige without contribution)
- AI-assisted writing must be disclosed per journal policy (ICMJE, WAME, COPE guidance)

## Reporting Standards

Apply the correct reporting guideline for each study type:

- **CONSORT** — Randomized Controlled Trials (including extensions for cluster, non-inferiority, pragmatic trials)
- **STROBE** — Observational studies (cohort, case-control, cross-sectional)
- **PRISMA** — Systematic reviews and meta-analyses
- **STARD** — Diagnostic accuracy studies
- **CARE** — Case reports
- **SPIRIT** — Clinical trial protocols
- **TRIPOD** — Prediction models (development and validation)
- **ARRIVE** — Animal research
- **CHEERS** — Health economic evaluations
- **SQUIRE** — Quality improvement studies
- Full checklist available at the EQUATOR Network (equator-network.org)

## Publication Ethics

- No **duplicate publication** — same data must not be published in multiple journals without disclosure
- No **salami slicing** — artificially splitting one study into multiple publications to inflate publication count
- No **selective reporting** — all pre-specified outcomes must be reported, including null results
- **Plagiarism**: all sources must be properly cited, including self-citation of prior work
- **Errata and retractions**: errors must be corrected promptly; fraudulent work must be retracted
- Follow **COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics)** flowcharts for ethical issues

## Statistical Integrity

- **Pre-specify analyses**: primary and secondary endpoints, statistical methods, sample size calculation
- **Declare post-hoc analyses** explicitly — never present exploratory findings as confirmatory
- **Report all outcomes**: including negative and inconclusive results (combat reporting bias)
- **Multiple comparisons**: apply appropriate correction (Bonferroni, Holm, FDR) or justify why not
- **P-value transparency**: report exact p-values, not just "p < 0.05"; consider confidence intervals and effect sizes
- **No p-hacking**: do not test multiple analyses and selectively report significant ones

## Research Ethics

- **Declaration of Helsinki**: ethical principles for medical research involving human subjects
- **Belmont Report** principles: Respect for Persons (autonomy, informed consent), Beneficence (maximize benefit, minimize harm), Justice (fair distribution of research burdens and benefits)
- **Informed consent**: voluntary, comprehensible, documented, with right to withdraw at any time
- **Vulnerable populations**: additional protections for children, prisoners, cognitively impaired, economically disadvantaged
- **Conflict of interest**: always disclose financial and non-financial COI; recommend COI statements in all publications

## Clinical Trial Registration

- All interventional trials must be **prospectively registered** before first participant enrollment:
  - ClinicalTrials.gov (US/international)
  - EudraCT / CTIS (EU)
  - DRKS (Germany)
  - ISRCTN (international)
- Registration must include primary outcome, sample size, and study design
- Results must be posted to registry within required timeframes (12 months per FDAAA 801, EU CTR requirements)

## Open Science

- Encourage **pre-registration** of study protocols and analysis plans (OSF, AsPredicted)
- Support **data sharing** where ethically and legally permissible (FAIR principles: Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable)
- Promote **open access** publication where appropriate
- Encourage use of **open-source tools** and reproducible analysis pipelines

## Quality Gate

Before finalizing any research output:
- [ ] Appropriate reporting guideline identified and referenced (CONSORT, STROBE, PRISMA, etc.)
- [ ] Authorship criteria verified (ICMJE)
- [ ] Conflicts of interest disclosed
- [ ] Statistical methods pre-specified or post-hoc status declared
- [ ] Ethical approval and informed consent documented
- [ ] Clinical trial registration verified (if interventional)
- [ ] AI involvement in writing disclosed
